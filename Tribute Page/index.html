Early life and education

  • 1) Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar, also known as Baba Saheb Ambedkar, was born on April 14, 1891, at Mhow in Madhya Pradesh, India.
  • 2) He was the fourteenth child of his parents. Ambedkar's father Ramji was a Subedar in the Indian Army and posted at Mhow cantonment.
  • 3) MR. Ambedkar had to face severe discriminations from every corner of the society as his parents hailed from the Hindu Mahar caste.
  • 4) Mahar cast was viewed as "untouchable" by the upper class. The discrimination and humiliation haunted Ambedkar even at the Army school, run by British government. Discrimination followed wherever he went.
  • 5) Early Education: Dr. Ambedkar began his education in a local school in Mhow, Madhya Pradesh.
  • 6) Scholarship Opportunities: He secured scholarships from the Maharaja of Baroda that enabled him to pursue higher education in Mumbai and later abroad. Elphinstone College: He graduated with a degree in Economics and Political Science from Elphinstone College, Mumbai, in 1912.
  • 7) Columbia University: Dr. Ambedkar pursued postgraduate studies in Economics and Political Science at Columbia University, earning M.A. and M.Sc. degrees in 1915 and 1916. London School of Economics: He conducted doctoral research at the London School of Economics and earned a Ph.D. for his thesis on "The Problem of the Rupee: Its Origin and Its Solution" in 1923. Law Studies: Upon returning to India, he studied law at Government Law College in Mumbai and obtained a law degree.
  • 8) Degrees: Dr. Ambedkar held degrees in Economics, Political Science, and Law, and he earned a Ph.D. for his research on monetary economics.

National recognition

  • 1) Champion of Dalit Rights: Dr. Ambedkar dedicated his life to advocating for the rights and dignity of Dalits. His leadership in the Dalit movement brought their struggles to the forefront of national discourse.
  • 2) Architect of the Indian Constitution: As the chairman of the Drafting Committee of the Constituent Assembly, Dr. Ambedkar played a pivotal role in crafting the Indian Constitution. His commitment to justice, equality, and fundamental rights is enshrined in the constitutional framework.
  • 3) Leader and Scholar: His scholarly achievements, including degrees in law, economics, and political science, showcased his intellectual prowess. His writings and speeches on social issues, human rights, and democracy resonated with people across the country..
  • 4) Anti-Caste Advocate: Dr. Ambedkar's unwavering critique of the caste system and his efforts to eradicate untouchability led to significant social reforms. He challenged traditional norms and practices, urging a more inclusive and egalitarian society..
  • 4) Mass Conversions: His call for mass conversions of Dalits to Buddhism highlighted his quest for an identity free from caste-based discrimination. These conversions garnered attention and highlighted the need for social reform. Legacy and Inspiration: Dr. Ambedkar's legacy continues to inspire political leaders, social activists, and marginalized communities. His life's work has had a transformative impact on modern India, fostering a more inclusive and just society.

International Success

  • 1) Scholarly Achievements: His academic pursuits and research in economics and law at renowned institutions like Columbia University and the London School of Economics earned him recognition in global academic circles.
  • 2) Leadership in Social Reform: His advocacy against caste-based discrimination and untouchability resonated beyond India, drawing attention to the struggle for equality and justice on the global stage.
  • 3) Constitutional Expertise: As the chief architect of India's Constitution, his work became a model for nascent democracies worldwide, showcasing his influence on constitutional and legal frameworks.

Personal life

  • 1) Name Change: His original name was Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar. He later dropped the first two names and adopted the name "B. R. Ambedkar." Education: Despite facing caste discrimination, he excelled in academics. He pursued studies in India and abroad, earning degrees in economics, political science, and law.
  • 2) Marriage: He married Ramabai Ambedkar in 1906. She was a source of support throughout his life and actively participated in social reform efforts.
  • 3) Children: Dr. Ambedkar and Ramabai had five children two sons and three daughters. Conversion to Buddhism: In 1956, along with a large number of followers, he converted to Buddhism, seeking an alternative to the caste-based discrimination prevalent in Hinduism.

Illness and death

  • 1) Illness and Health Struggles: Dr. Ambedkar suffered from various health issues, including diabetes and weak eyesight. His health was often affected by the stress and demands of his relentless social and political activities..
  • 2) Last Years: In his later years, his health deteriorated further due to his demanding schedule and persistent health problems. Death: On December 6, 1956, at the age of 65, Dr. B. R. Ambedkar passed away at his home in Delhi. He had been bedridden for several days due to health complications.
  • 3) Legacy: Dr. Ambedkar's death was a significant loss to the nation. His contributions to social justice, human rights, and the drafting of the Indian Constitution left an indelible mark on India's history.
